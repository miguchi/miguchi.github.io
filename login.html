Login and Registration

As part of the digital education start-up like branch of Perfection Learning, I was tasked with redesigning login and registration for all digital math and science products.

The last release of login and registration had caused confusion and frustration from students, teachers, and sales representatives for years. Since the last release, new state regulations (FERPA) had developed that protected the security of students. This limited the amount of information that we could collect for students; in some states, we were not allowed to use student emails as login credentials for the product.

How might we make the registration and sign in process smoother for customers, while following FERPA requirements?

Role
user experience designer (UX), product design

Tools
Sketch, Axure, Bootstrap (HTML/CSS), paper and pencil, Keynote

Responsibilities
End-to-end feature design from the initial email sent to customers to register for the product to signing in for the product. Deliverables included:
user goals
competitive analysis/market research
user flows
information architecture
sketches
prototype
usability testing
wireframes
responsive HTML/CSS templates for use in the final product

Requirements
Allow method for students to use usernames instead of emails for sign in (FERPA)
Ensure password security for students
Streamline sign in and registration process
Usable in multiple product lines for teachers and students
Use as much of the existing API as possible

User Goals

Administrator Goals

  • Administrator buys product for entire school and logs in for the first time
  • Administrator logs in once per day
  • Administrator forgets username or password

Teacher Goals

  • Teacher logs in to create account
  • Teacher is using several products and needs to register for all of them
  • Teacher who has used product in the past, but now needs to acccess a different product
  • Teacher forgets username or password
  • Teacher logs into product on a daily basis

Students Goals

  • Student logs into use product for the first time
  • Student forgets username or password
  • Student used the product for a different class and is using it again for a new class

Competitive/Market Analysis

When completing the competitive analysis, the most striking thing about other products in the market place was that there was usually one pathway for student account creation. We were allowing for two methods for student account creation: teachers to create accounts for students and students to create accounts for themselves. The need to cover all use-cases was bloating our product. I pitched and negotiated removing one pathway: the ability for students to create their own accounts. This was validated by discussions with school IT staff and our marketing team, who stated that our target purchasers, districts and schools, want control over creating student accounts.

User Flow

User flows were developed centering around several requirements:

  1. Adults create their own accounts and upload student information to create student accounts.
  2. Adults cannot create student passwords. For security reasons, students must create their own passwords.
  3. Students must have the flexibility to have a username or email address (but not both).
  4. Adults must sign in with email addresses.

Sketches and Information Architecture

One of the blocks in the previous design was the form length. Users were getting stuck filling out the form and were not being notified on their progress. Focusing the forms to one input field would, counterintuitively, remove friction. After discussions with the development team on feasability options, I recommended single screens with one input field and inline validation. I then followed the user flows to develop one screen interactions for all use-cases for logging in and registering.

Prototype, Wireframes, and Usability Testing

Using Axure, I created a low-fidelity, medium-function prototype to test my design hypothesis on users. Click here to see the prototype. I tested the old system versus the new system, and increased the SUS usability of registration and login from 22.5% to 92.5%.

A use-case I tested was (and you can test in the prototype):
It is the first day of school and your teacher wants you to set up your math homework product.
Your teacher hands you a slip of paper that says:
Login at math.com.
Code: animalapple
Username: lisafrank1

Responsive Design with HTML/CSS Templates

After researching, prototyping, and testing, I used the basic wireframes from the prototype to create HTML/CSS responsive templates using Bootstrap. For the interaction, I wanted to focus on one panel that would contain each single text input field, next and back buttons, along with validation messages and branding. I kept the input field the same distance from the brand for continuity of clicks into the input field.
